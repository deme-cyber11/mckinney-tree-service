How Much Does Tree Service Cost in McKinney? (2026 Guide)

March 29, 2026

McKinney is one of the fastest-growing suburbs in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex, and with that growth comes thousands of mature residential lots filled with cedar elm, live oak, pecan, and post oak trees. Whether you just moved into an established neighborhood near Historic Downtown or you are maintaining a newer build in Stonebridge Ranch or Tucker Hill, understanding what tree service costs in McKinney helps you plan your budget and avoid surprises when you call for an estimate.

Below is an updated 2026 pricing guide based on real-world rates from tree service contractors working throughout McKinney, TX and the greater Collin County area.

Average Tree Service Costs in McKinney, TX

The table below shows typical price ranges for the most common tree services requested by McKinney homeowners. Actual costs depend on tree size, species, location on your property, and accessibility.

Factors That Affect Tree Service Costs

No two tree jobs are identical, and several factors specific to McKinney and Collin County can push your price up or down.

  • Tree height and canopy spread: A 70-foot live oak with a 50-foot canopy takes significantly more time, labor, and equipment than a 20-foot ornamental crape myrtle. Height is the single biggest cost driver for both removal and trimming.
  • Species and wood density: Cedar elm and post oak are common throughout McKinney and are dense hardwoods that take longer to cut, chip, and haul. Softer species like Bradford pear cost less to process.
  • Proximity to structures: Trees growing close to your home, fence, pool, or power lines require careful rigging and directional felling. Many McKinney neighborhoods have homes built on compact lots where trees are within feet of the foundation, adding complexity to every job.
  • Collin County clay soil: The heavy clay soil in McKinney affects root systems and can make stump grinding more labor-intensive. It also contributes to foundation proximity issues, which is often the reason homeowners request tree removal in the first place.
  • Accessibility: If the crew cannot get a bucket truck or chipper into the backyard, the job requires more hand-carrying and climbing, which adds labor hours.
  • Season and weather: McKinney sits in North Texas hail alley, with severe storm season running from March through June. Emergency calls during and after storms carry a premium because of urgency and high demand.
  • Number of trees: Bundling multiple trees or services into a single visit typically reduces the per-tree cost since the crew and equipment are already on-site.

What Should Be Included in Your Estimate

A reputable tree service company in McKinney should provide a detailed written estimate that covers the full scope of work. Before you sign anything, make sure the quote includes:

  • Felling or trimming: The actual cutting work, including how the tree will be taken down (crane, rigging, or freefall).
  • Debris removal and hauling: All cut wood, branches, and leaves should be chipped and hauled away unless you specifically want to keep the wood.
  • Stump grinding (if applicable): Some companies include stump grinding in the removal price. Others quote it separately. Always ask.
  • Yard cleanup: The crew should rake and blow your yard clean when the job is done. You should not have to clean up sawdust, wood chips, and leaf debris yourself.
  • Proof of insurance: Liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage should be current and verifiable. Ask for a certificate of insurance before work begins.

How to Choose a Tree Service in McKinney

Hiring the right tree service contractor matters just as much as getting a fair price. Here is what to look for:

  • Local reputation: Choose a company with a track record in McKinney and Collin County. Local contractors understand the soil conditions, common tree species, and municipal regulations.
  • Insurance and licensing: Verify that the company carries both general liability insurance and workers' compensation. This protects you from financial responsibility if something goes wrong on your property.
  • Written estimates: A professional company will walk your property, assess every tree, and give you a written quote with a clear scope of work. Be cautious of anyone who quotes a price over the phone without seeing the job.
  • No large deposits: Reputable tree service companies do not require large upfront payments. A small deposit is reasonable for large jobs, but never pay the full amount before work is complete.
  • Avoid storm chasers: After severe weather, out-of-town crews often show up offering low prices. These operators frequently lack insurance, deliver subpar work, and disappear if problems arise.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to remove a tree in McKinney, TX?

Tree removal in McKinney typically costs between $250 and $3,000 or more depending on the size and location of the tree. Small trees under 30 feet cost $250 to $700, medium trees (30 to 60 feet) cost $700 to $1,500, and large trees over 60 feet cost $1,500 to $3,000 or more. Trees near structures, power lines, or in hard-to-access areas cost more.

Does McKinney require a permit to remove a tree?

McKinney has a tree preservation ordinance that may require a permit for removing trees above a certain trunk diameter, particularly protected species. Your tree service contractor should be familiar with local regulations and can help you determine whether a permit is needed before work begins.

What is the best time of year to have trees trimmed in McKinney?

Late fall through early spring (November to February) is generally the best time to trim most tree species in McKinney. Trees are dormant during this period, which reduces stress and lowers the risk of disease transmission. However, dead or hazardous branches should be removed any time of year for safety.

Does insurance cover tree removal in McKinney?

Homeowners insurance typically covers tree removal when a tree falls on a structure (your home, garage, or fence) due to a covered peril like a storm. However, it usually does not cover removal of a standing dead tree or a tree that falls in your yard without damaging a structure. Check with your insurance provider for your specific policy details.

Why do tree service prices vary so much between companies?

Price differences come down to insurance coverage, equipment quality, crew experience, and what is included in the quote. A lower price may not include stump grinding, debris hauling, or cleanup. Always compare the full scope of work, verify the company carries liability insurance and workers' compensation, and check reviews before hiring.
