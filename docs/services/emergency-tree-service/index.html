When Trees Become an Immediate Danger

North Texas weather does not wait for business hours. Severe thunderstorms, straight-line winds, tornadoes, and ice storms can snap trunks, uproot root systems, and send massive limbs crashing onto homes, vehicles, fences, and power lines without warning. When that happens, you need a tree crew that answers the phone at 2 a.m. and arrives ready to work. McKinney Tree Pros provides true 24/7 emergency tree service across McKinney and all surrounding communities — no voicemail, no callbacks tomorrow, no waiting.

Our emergency response team carries chainsaws, rigging gear, a chipper, and a crane-ready truck so we can handle virtually any situation on arrival. Whether a mature oak has split and is leaning against your roofline, a fallen tree is blocking your driveway, or a dangling limb is tangled in power lines, we stabilize the situation, make it safe, and complete the removal as quickly as conditions allow. We coordinate with utility companies and local emergency services when downed power lines or structural damage are involved.

Speed matters during tree emergencies, but so does safety. A partially fallen tree under tension can shift unpredictably; a hanging branch can release without warning. Our crews are trained to read these situations, identify load paths, and use controlled rigging techniques to bring hazardous material down without causing additional damage to your property. We carry full liability insurance, so you are protected even in high-stakes, high-pressure scenarios.

After the immediate hazard is cleared, we handle all follow-up work: full tree removal if needed, debris hauling, stump grinding, and coordination with your insurance adjuster. We provide detailed documentation, including photos and written assessments, to support your homeowners insurance claim. Most insurance policies cover storm damage tree removal — and we will help you navigate the process from start to finish.

Emergency Situations We Handle

  • Storm-damaged trees — Split trunks, uprooted trees, and snapped branches after severe weather.
  • Trees on structures — Fallen trees resting on roofs, garages, sheds, carports, and fences.
  • Trees on vehicles — Safe removal without causing additional damage to cars and trucks.
  • Blocked driveways and roads — Rapid clearing to restore access to your property.
  • Power line hazards — Coordination with Oncor and utility crews for safe limb removal near live lines.
  • Hanging and dangling limbs — Removal of widow-makers and partially detached branches before they fall.
