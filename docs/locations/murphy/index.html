Our Services in Murphy

Tree Removal — Murphy's compact residential lots mean trees that outgrow their space can quickly become a concern. Our removal specialists take a measured approach, carefully dismantling trees in sections when proximity to homes, fences, or power lines demands it, and hauling away all debris when the job is done.

Tree Trimming — Keeping trees properly trimmed is especially important in Murphy, where homes are often close together and overhanging branches can encroach on neighboring properties. We prune for health, safety, and aesthetics, ensuring every cut promotes strong future growth.

Stump Grinding — On Murphy's smaller lots, a leftover stump takes up valuable yard space. Our stump grinding service removes the stump below the soil surface, eliminating a tripping hazard and freeing up room for new plantings, a patio extension, or simply more open lawn.

Emergency Tree Service — When a storm drops a limb on your Murphy home or a weakened tree suddenly falls, you need help fast. Our emergency response team arrives equipped to remove the hazard, tarp any exposed areas, and restore safety to your property as quickly as possible.

Tree Health Assessment — Murphy homeowners who notice unusual leaf drop, bark damage, or fungal growth on their trees can call us for a thorough assessment. We diagnose the issue, explain your options, and recommend a treatment plan or, if necessary, safe removal.

Lot Clearing — Whether you are clearing a backyard for a major landscaping renovation or preparing an undeveloped Murphy lot for construction, we remove trees, brush, and stumps with efficiency and care, leaving the ground ready for your next project.

About Murphy

Murphy is a small, predominantly residential city in southern Collin County, bordered by Plano, Wylie, and Sachse. Despite its compact size of roughly six square miles, Murphy has earned a reputation as one of the most desirable places to live in the Dallas-Fort Worth area, thanks to its excellent Plano ISD schools, low crime rates, and strong community engagement. The city's neighborhoods feature a mature tree canopy dominated by live oaks, red oaks, and crape myrtles that line residential streets and fill backyards. Because lots in Murphy tend to be moderate in size, professional tree maintenance is critical to preventing overgrowth, reducing storm damage risk, and keeping the neighborhood looking its best. McKinney Tree Pros is proud to help Murphy homeowners protect and enhance the trees that make their community so attractive.
