How Much Does Tree Service Cost in McKinney, TX?

March 22, 2026

If you own a home in McKinney, TX, chances are you have at least a few trees on your property. Whether you need a dead tree removed, branches trimmed back from your roof, or a stubborn stump ground down, understanding what tree service costs in this area helps you budget wisely and avoid overpaying. Below is a detailed breakdown of typical pricing for the most common tree services in McKinney and Collin County.

Tree Removal Costs: $400 – $2,500

Tree removal is one of the most requested services we provide, and pricing varies widely based on the size and condition of the tree. A small ornamental tree under 25 feet tall may cost as little as $400 to remove, while a large oak or pecan tree over 60 feet can run $1,500 to $2,500 or more. Mid-size trees between 25 and 50 feet typically fall in the $800 to $1,500 range. These estimates include felling the tree, cutting it into manageable sections, and hauling away the debris. If the tree is in a tight space near your home, fence, or power lines, expect the cost to be on the higher end because the crew needs specialized rigging equipment and additional safety precautions.

Tree Trimming Costs: $200 – $1,000

Routine tree trimming is essential for keeping your trees healthy and your property safe. For a single small to mid-size tree, most McKinney homeowners pay between $200 and $500. Larger trees or those that require climbing and extensive canopy reduction can cost $600 to $1,000. Regular trimming every one to three years is far more affordable than waiting until branches become a hazard. Crown thinning, deadwood removal, and clearance pruning over rooflines and driveways are the most common trimming jobs we handle in the McKinney area.

Stump Grinding Costs: $150 – $500

After a tree is removed, the stump left behind can be an eyesore, a tripping hazard, and an invitation for pests. Stump grinding typically costs between $150 and $500 depending on the diameter of the stump and root flare. Most stumps in the 12- to 24-inch range fall around $200 to $300. If you have multiple stumps, many contractors, including our team, offer discounted rates when grinding several stumps in a single visit.

Emergency Tree Service Costs

Storms are a fact of life in North Texas. When a tree falls on your roof or blocks your driveway after severe weather, emergency tree service is available around the clock. Emergency work typically costs 25% to 50% more than standard removal because of the urgency, after-hours labor, and sometimes hazardous working conditions. While it costs more, prompt removal prevents further damage to your property and keeps your family safe.

Factors That Affect Tree Service Pricing

No two jobs are exactly alike. Here are the key factors that influence what you will pay:

  • Tree size and height: Taller, larger trees require more labor, heavier equipment, and more time to safely remove or trim.
  • Location on your property: Trees near structures, fences, power lines, or pools require careful rigging and take longer to work on safely.
  • Accessibility: If the tree is in a backyard with no gate access for equipment, crews may need to carry debris by hand, adding to labor costs.
  • Tree species: Hardwoods like post oak and pecan are denser and heavier than softwoods, which means more effort and equipment to process.
  • Condition of the tree: Dead, diseased, or storm-damaged trees can be unpredictable and may require extra safety measures.
  • Number of trees: Bundling multiple trees or services into one visit often results in a lower per-tree cost.

Tips for Getting a Fair Price

Getting the best value on tree service does not mean choosing the cheapest bid. Here are some practical tips:

  • Get at least two or three estimates so you can compare scope of work, not just price.
  • Ask what is included. Does the quote cover debris removal, stump grinding, and cleanup? Or are those extra?
  • Verify insurance. A company without liability insurance and workers' compensation puts you at financial risk if something goes wrong.
  • Avoid storm chasers. After severe weather, out-of-town crews may offer low prices but deliver poor work with no accountability.
  • Schedule during the off-season. Winter months in North Texas are often slower for tree service companies, which can mean better availability and pricing.
