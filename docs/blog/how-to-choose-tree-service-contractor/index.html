How to Choose a Tree Service Contractor

March 22, 2026

Hiring a tree service company is not something most McKinney homeowners do every day. Whether you need a large tree removed, routine trimming, or storm damage cleanup, choosing the right contractor makes the difference between a job done safely and professionally and one that leaves you with property damage, liability, or an unfinished mess. Here is what to look for and what to avoid when hiring a tree service contractor in McKinney, TX.

Verify Insurance and Licensing

This is the single most important step you can take, and it is the one most homeowners skip. Tree work is inherently dangerous. Heavy limbs, chainsaws, aerial lifts, and proximity to power lines and structures mean that accidents can and do happen. A legitimate tree service company should carry both general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. General liability protects your property if something goes wrong during the job. Workers' compensation protects you from being held financially responsible if a crew member is injured on your property.

Do not just take the contractor's word for it. Ask to see a current certificate of insurance and verify it is valid by calling the insurance provider directly. In Texas, tree service companies are not required to hold a state license, which makes insurance verification even more critical. Any reputable company will be happy to provide proof of coverage without hesitation.

Get Multiple Estimates

You should always get at least two or three written estimates before committing to a tree service provider. A thorough estimate should include the specific work to be performed, the total cost, what is included in that cost (debris removal, stump grinding, cleanup), and an approximate timeline. Comparing multiple quotes helps you understand the fair market price for your job and lets you evaluate how each company communicates and presents itself.

Be cautious of estimates that are dramatically lower than others. While everyone loves a deal, a bid that comes in far below the competition often means the company is cutting corners on insurance, equipment, or safety standards. On the other hand, the most expensive bid is not automatically the best. Focus on the value and professionalism behind the number, not just the number itself.

Check Reviews and References

Online reviews on Google, Yelp, and the Better Business Bureau give you a window into how a company operates day to day. Look for patterns in the reviews rather than fixating on any single comment. Consistent praise for punctuality, clean workmanship, and clear communication is a strong indicator of a reliable contractor. Recurring complaints about missed appointments, hidden charges, or unfinished work are red flags.

Beyond online reviews, do not hesitate to ask the company for references from recent local jobs. A reputable tree service will have no problem connecting you with satisfied customers in the McKinney area who can speak to the quality of their work firsthand.

Ask About Equipment and Process

A professional tree service company invests in proper equipment and follows established safety procedures. When you speak with a potential contractor, ask about the equipment they will use for your specific job. For large tree removals, you should expect them to use bucket trucks, cranes or rigging systems, and commercial-grade chippers rather than just a pickup truck and a chainsaw. Ask how they plan to protect your lawn, driveway, and landscaping during the work, and confirm that cleanup and debris hauling are included in the estimate.

It is also worth asking whether the company employs certified arborists or has crew members with formal training in tree care. While certification is not legally required, it demonstrates a commitment to industry best practices and ongoing education.

Red Flags to Watch For

Knowing what to avoid is just as important as knowing what to look for. Here are the most common warning signs of a disreputable tree service contractor:

  • No proof of insurance: If a company cannot or will not provide a certificate of insurance, walk away. You could be held liable for injuries or property damage that occur during the job.
  • Door-to-door solicitation: Be wary of crews that show up at your door unsolicited, especially after a storm. These are often transient operators, sometimes called "storm chasers," who move from town to town, collect payment, and deliver substandard work with no accountability.
  • Demands full payment upfront: A reasonable deposit for materials or scheduling is normal, but a company that demands the full amount before any work begins is a major red flag. Reputable contractors are paid upon completion or according to an agreed-upon milestone schedule.
  • No written estimate: Verbal agreements leave room for misunderstandings and price increases. Always insist on a written, itemized estimate before work begins.
  • Wants to top your trees: Tree topping, the practice of cutting large branches back to stubs, is widely condemned by arborists as harmful to tree health and structural integrity. A knowledgeable contractor will recommend proper pruning techniques instead.
