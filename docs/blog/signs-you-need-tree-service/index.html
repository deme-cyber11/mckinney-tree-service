5 Signs You Need Professional Tree Service

March 22, 2026

Trees are one of the best features of any McKinney property. They provide shade in the brutal Texas summers, increase your home's curb appeal, and can even boost property value. But trees are living organisms, and like anything alive, they can get sick, become damaged, or grow in ways that create serious hazards. The key to protecting your property and your family is knowing when a tree needs professional attention. Here are five warning signs every homeowner should watch for.

1. Dead or Hanging Branches

Dead branches are the most obvious sign that your tree needs professional care. Sometimes called "widow makers" in the tree care industry, dead limbs can snap and fall without warning, especially during North Texas wind storms and severe weather events. Look for branches that have no leaves during the growing season, bark that is peeling away, or limbs that appear dry and brittle compared to the rest of the canopy. Hanging branches that are partially broken but still attached to the tree are especially dangerous and should be addressed immediately. Our tree trimming service can safely remove deadwood and hanging limbs before they cause damage to your roof, vehicle, or fence.

2. Leaning Trunk

While some trees naturally grow at a slight angle, a tree that has recently started leaning is a serious cause for concern. A sudden lean often indicates root failure, soil erosion, or structural weakness at the base of the trunk. After heavy rains, which are common in the McKinney area during spring, saturated soil can loosen root systems and cause previously stable trees to shift. If you notice a tree leaning more than it used to, or if you see cracked or heaving soil on one side of the base, call a professional right away. In many cases, the tree will need to be removed before it falls on its own, which could cause catastrophic damage to your home or neighboring property.

3. Visible Disease or Fungus

Trees in the McKinney area are susceptible to a number of diseases, including oak wilt, hypoxylon canker, and bacterial leaf scorch. Warning signs include unusual leaf discoloration or early leaf drop, dark oozing spots on the bark, mushrooms or shelf fungi growing at the base of the tree or along the trunk, and areas of soft, spongy wood. Fungal growth at the base of a tree is particularly concerning because it often indicates internal decay that compromises the structural integrity of the trunk. A tree health assessment from a trained arborist can identify the specific problem and determine whether the tree can be treated or needs to be removed to prevent the disease from spreading to nearby trees.

4. Root Damage or Heaving

A tree's root system is its foundation. When roots are damaged by construction, soil compaction, changes in drainage patterns, or severe weather, the entire tree is at risk. Signs of root problems include visible roots that are severed, decayed, or lifting out of the ground; cracked or heaving soil in a ring around the base of the tree; and a noticeable decline in the tree's overall health, such as thinning foliage or smaller-than-usual leaves. Root damage is often hidden underground, which is why it is one of the most dangerous issues a homeowner can face. If you have recently had construction, landscaping, or drainage work done near a large tree and are noticing any of these symptoms, schedule a professional evaluation as soon as possible.

5. Overgrown Canopy

An overgrown canopy might not seem as urgent as a dead branch or a leaning trunk, but it creates problems that compound over time. Branches that press against your roof can damage shingles and provide a highway for pests like squirrels and rats to enter your attic. Limbs that hang over driveways and sidewalks create liability if they fall on a person or vehicle. Dense canopies also block sunlight and airflow, which stresses your lawn and can promote fungal growth within the tree itself. Regular pruning and trimming keeps your trees in proportion with your property, improves air circulation through the canopy, and reduces the weight on heavy limbs that are prone to breaking during storms.

When in Doubt, Call a Professional

If you have noticed any of these signs on your property, do not wait for the problem to get worse. A falling tree or large branch can cause tens of thousands of dollars in damage to your home, and the risk to your family's safety is not worth the gamble.
